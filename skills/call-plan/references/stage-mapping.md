# AWS Sales Stage Mapping

> Reference: GCR Field Enablement — AWS Sales Stage Advancement Standards. Each stage defines exit criteria, key qualification elements (MEDDPICC-derived), sales activities, and common pitfalls.
>
> This is the **single source of truth** for stage definitions.

---

## Prospect

| Dimension | Details |
|---|---|
| **Exit Criteria** | Customer opportunity identified; valid estimated value; initial meeting scheduled |
| **Key MEDDPICC Elements** | **I** (Pain): Identify pain points & compelling events · **E** (Economic Buyer): Initial budget direction · **M** (Metrics): Evidence-based opportunity valuation · **DP** (Decision Process): Initiate engagement · **Ch** (Champion): Assess potential champion |
| **Sales Activities** | Account research and profiling · Identify key stakeholders and entry points · Initial outreach (cold/warm) · First meeting / introductory call · Validate opportunity: is there a real pain point? · Assess potential champion · Understand org structure and reporting lines · Deliver initial credibility (industry insights, peer references) · Propose next step to advance to Qualified |
| **Common Pitfalls** | Pushing for commitment too early · Talking more than listening · Skipping account research · Single-threading (only one contact) |

---

## Qualified

| Dimension | Details |
|---|---|
| **Exit Criteria** | Customer willing to pursue solution; champion & decision-maker identified; delivery partner strategy identified |
| **Key MEDDPICC Elements** | **I**: Validated pain, buyer's desired business outcomes · **M**: Initial business success metrics defined · **Ch**: Internal supporter confirmed · **DP**: Budget and timeframe clear · **E**: Final decision-maker identified · **CP** (Competition): Competitive strategy determined |
| **Sales Activities** | Deep discovery across stakeholders · Validate and quantify pain points · Map full stakeholder landscape (champion, EB, influencers, blockers) · Understand decision process, timeline, and budget · Identify and assess competitive landscape · Define initial success metrics with customer · Develop value proposition tailored to customer's pain · Identify delivery partner if applicable · Build internal business case for AWS investment |
| **Common Pitfalls** | Presenting solutions before fully understanding requirements · Failing to multi-thread (relying on single champion) · Not validating budget and timeline early · Ignoring competitive dynamics |

## Technical Validation

| Dimension | Details |
|---|---|
| **Exit Criteria** | Shared understanding of architecture/implementation; technical solution finalized and agreed |
| **Key MEDDPICC Elements** | **DC** (Decision Criteria): Technical requirements agreed · **DP**: Implementation process confirmed · **M**: Technical success metrics established · **Ch**: Technical champion alignment secured |
| **Sales Activities** | Architecture design and review · Demo / technical deep-dive · POC planning and execution (jointly define scope and success criteria with customer) · Technical workshop · Address integration constraints and compliance requirements · Validate scalability and performance · Identify and mitigate technical blockers · Secure technical champion alignment · Document agreed technical approach |
| **Common Pitfalls** | Defining POC success criteria unilaterally (without customer input) · Ignoring integration complexity · Not identifying technical blockers early · Losing business stakeholder engagement during technical phase |

---

## Business Validation

| Dimension | Details |
|---|---|
| **Exit Criteria** | Customer approvals secured; deal details/economics/close plan agreed |
| **Key MEDDPICC Elements** | **E**: Final decision-maker approval · **DP**: Close date confirmed · **PP** (Paper Process): Procurement steps understood · **M**: Financial metrics confirmed · **E**: Commercial terms aligned |
| **Sales Activities** | Build and present business case (ROI, TCO) · Executive alignment meetings · Pricing proposal and negotiation · Map full procurement process (approval chain, legal, security, contract) · Address commercial objections with value-based responses · Secure economic buyer sign-off · Align on contract terms and timeline · Coordinate with procurement and legal |
| **Common Pitfalls** | Discounting without value justification · Not understanding the full procurement process · Bypassing procurement (they will find out) · Presenting vague ROI without specific assumptions and numbers |

---

## Committed

| Dimension | Details |
|---|---|
| **Exit Criteria** | Agreement signed (PPA); migration started or MRR 10% usage (GCR Practice: 80%) |
| **Key MEDDPICC Elements** | **M**: Migration plan meets business metrics · **DC**: Deployment timeline confirmed · **PP**: Contract terms negotiated and executed · **E**: Final decision-maker contract confirmation |
| **Sales Activities** | Contract finalization and signing · Kick-off planning · Migration / implementation initiation · Establish success metrics and tracking · Transition from sales to delivery/customer success · Monitor initial adoption and usage · Address early implementation concerns quickly |
| **Common Pitfalls** | "Throwing it over the wall" to delivery without transition plan · Not monitoring early adoption · Ignoring customer concerns post-signature · Missing expansion signals |

---

## Closed/Launched

| Dimension | Details |
|---|---|
| **Exit Criteria** | Opportunity advanced to final stage — deal won |
| **Key MEDDPICC Elements** | **DP**: Project decision process requirements met · **M**: Post-implementation business metrics achievable · **DC**: Project decision criteria met · **PP**: Delivery documentation completed |
| **Sales Activities** | Implementation review and optimization · Customer success check-ins (QBR) · Measure and report on business outcomes · Identify expansion opportunities (new workloads, new BUs, new regions, upsell, cross-sell) · Renewal planning · Build customer reference / case study · Address unresolved concerns before pursuing expansion |
| **Common Pitfalls** | Pushing expansion before current implementation is healthy · Not measuring or reporting on promised business outcomes · Losing executive relationships after the deal closes · Treating the customer as "done" instead of "growing" |

---

## Usage

Use this mapping to:
1. **Focus Call Plan discovery questions** on the qualification elements most critical for the current stage advancement
2. **Align Target Outcomes** with the stage exit criteria — what must be true to advance?
3. **Guide sales activities** — what should the rep be doing at this stage?
4. **Identify gaps** blocking stage progression
5. **Suggest stage advancement** when enough elements are confirmed
6. **Coach on common pitfalls** — what mistakes are typical at this stage?
